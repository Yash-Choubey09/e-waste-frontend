Environmental Impacts

Toxic Substances

E-waste contains hazardous materials like lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants that can leach into soil and water supplies.

Water Contamination

Heavy metals from e-waste can seep into groundwater, making it unsafe for drinking and harming aquatic ecosystems.

Air Pollution

Burning e-waste releases toxic fumes containing dioxins and furans, contributing to air pollution and climate change.

Health Impacts

Respiratory Problems

Inhalation of toxic fumes from burning e-waste can cause lung damage and respiratory diseases.

Neurological Damage

Exposure to lead and mercury can impair brain development in children and cause neurological disorders.

DNA Damage

Some e-waste components are carcinogenic and can cause DNA damage leading to cancer.
